Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

"Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper, creating a creamy and savory flavor."

Ingredients

  • 200g spaghetti
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until al dente, according to the package instructions. Reserve some pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat a pan over medium heat and cook the pancetta (or guanciale) until it is crispy. If using, add the garlic cloves for flavor, then remove them once fragrant.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese until well combined. Season with a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Remove the pan with pancetta from heat and add the drained spaghetti to it. Toss to coat the pasta with the pancetta fat.
  5. Quickly add the egg and cheese mixture to the pan, tossing the pasta continuously to create a creamy sauce. Add a splash of reserved pasta water if needed to achieve the desired consistency.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with extra cheese, black pepper, and chopped parsley if desired.
